Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is Congenital Dyserythropoyetic Anemia?
Congenital dyserythropoietic anemia (CDA) is a rare blood disorder, similar to the thalassemias. CDA is a type of anemia that is characterized for an ineffective erythropoiesis (they are not generated red blood cells properly), intermittent jaundice, hepatosplenomegaly, iron overload and presents some morphological features characteristic of erythroblasts.
How can I know if I or any relative suffer from CDA?
We would like to encourage you to visit your doctor and ask for a blood-test. Your doctor can use the tools provided in this web in order to have a preliminary diagnostic. Make sure you have available information regarding hemoglobin level, reticulocytes count, platelets count and Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV).
After obtaining the result using the diagnostic algorithm of the web, how can I know where a molecular genetic diagnosis can be conducted?
After the algorithm tells you the result or clinical suspicion, it shows two buttons in the lower part: the one on the right allows you to make a new diagnosis and the one on the left will send you to NCBI's Genetic Testing Registry (GTR) page, where you can get information about which laboratories perform these CDA molecular diagnostic tests.
Why do I need to provide information regarding my gender and age?
This information is used by the algorithm to establish the threshold of the normal values for each required parameter. Gender and age are important because the values may vary. You can check the parameters used by the algorithm here.
