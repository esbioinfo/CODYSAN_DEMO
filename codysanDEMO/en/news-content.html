CoDysAn Project

Congenital dyserythropoietic anemia (CDA) is a heterogeneous group of inherited hematological disorders. Patients have chronic anemia of variable degree due to poor development of red blood cells (inefficient erythropoiesis), and morphological defects of erythrocyte precursors in the bone marrow. Other frequent clinical manifestations are jaundice, enlargement of the spleen (splenomegaly) and / or liver (hepatomegaly). The first symptoms usually appear during childhood or neonatal period. Patients require repeated blood transfusions in order to stabilize the anemia. Currently there are 5 subtypes of CDA described that are caused by mutations in different genes. In the laboratory of Dr. Mayka Sanchez a panel of genes related to iron metabolism has been developed, including genes described in the different subtypes of CDA, to improve molecular diagnosis in these patients. In addition, the identification of the mutated gene allows a more adequate treatment to be applied in affected individuals.

Now, thanks to the support of the Association of relatives and patients with congenital dyserythropoietic anemia (ADISCON) and the Pablo Ugarte Association (APU), the group of Dr. Mayka Sanchez has been able to hire two young researchers to carry out the project. "Towards an improvement in the diagnosis and treatment of congenital dyserythropoietic anemia type 2". The collaboration of groups of patients and society is essential to continue research into rare diseases, as is the case of CDA, since public funds have been reduced in recent years.

Laura Zalba, Researcher in the CoDysAn project

I decided to study the degree of Genetics because it was one of the subjects that attracted me the most about biology during high school. In addition, my professor emphasized the importance that this field would have in the study of human diseases. What I did not imagine is that something that started as "well, let's see how it goes", was going to fascinate me so much. I have a great interest in the study of human genetics and I am happy knowing that the work I do in research can help to understand a little better the molecular mechanisms of many human diseases, being able to fine-tune in their genetic diagnosis and get to improve the quality of life of patients. I firmly know that my future lies in research; to continue feeding my curiosity, to understand a little better where we come from, why we are what we are or what makes us equal but at the same time different. So, I thank the APU Foundation and ADISCON to make my dream possible and be able to be remunerated through their donations.

Laura Zalba has a Genetic degree from the Autonomous University of Barcelona and a master's degree in Translational Medicine from the University of Barcelona.

Marc Vila Cuenca, Postdoctoral Researcher CoDysAn project

I decided to study a Degree in Biochemistry and a Master's Degree in Cell Biology at the Autonomous University of Barcelona to understand more about the alterations that occur at the cellular and molecular level of different diseases.

At the Vrije Universitet in Amsterdam I had the opportunity to do my PhD in endothelial dysfunction associated with chronic kidney disease and thanks to this experience I was able to expand my knowledge in different scientific fields related to inflammation, vascular disease and cell dysfunction. My goal now is to continue learning new methodologies and continue research to contribute to improving the diagnosis and treatment of patients. Thanks to ADISCON, I can be part of the group of Dr. Mayka Sanchez, continue contributing my grain of sand to research in hematological disorders and especially help patients with congenital dyserythropoietic type 2 anemia.
